My Philosophy

When you ask a college admissions officer what they look for and what the ~ secret formula ~ is to get into their top-tier program, you always get the same answer.

"Just be yourself. There is no formula"
-- Every College Admissions Officer Ever

Now if you were anything like me growing up, you probably shrugged off this piece of advice as a cop-out answer. What do they mean "Just be yourself"? If there is NO formula, then why does it seem like every person who gets into XYZ college was involved with [insert a long list of clubs here], played [insert several instruments here], and held leadership positions in [insert some combination of student council, robotics, science olympiad, MUN, etc.]??

Well, my dear reader, not everything is as it seems on the surface.

It just so happens (to no one's surprise), the college admissions officers are actually completely correct and honest in their advice. While it does seem like an overwhelming number of people who get into great colleges fit a similar profile, an even greater number of people who fit a similar profile DO NOT get into such colleges.

So no. You are not guaranteed admission to [insert excellent college here] if you fit a particular profile.

If, however, you put forth the best and fullest representation of yourself in your application, you ARE guaranteeing that every college admitting you will be an excellent fit for you and your endeavors. And you WILL be successful at that school. So while I cannot promise you that working with me will land you in an Ivy+ school, I CAN promise that I will work my hardest to ensure that the application you submit represents your authentic self in the best way possible.

My Services

Common Application

The Common Application has two major components that you should use to showcase your wonderful character and abilities: the essay and extracurricular section. These components to the application must be specific to you, but need not be specific to the school.

Interview Prep

Interviewing is a skill. Like any other skill, you can improve with practice, more practice, and even more practice.

Supplemental Essay Review

The supplemental essays are a further chance to show schools what you're made of and who you are but more importantly, it's a chance for you to talk about why you are interested in a specific school and why that specific school should offer you a place in their class!

Extra Curricular Planning

Sometimes we need some help from others to learn how to make the most of our high school experience. I will never tell you what activities to do to get into XYZ school. That's not how this works. We should always be learning, pushing ourselves, and preparing ourselves for the next step. It just so happens certain activities can help you explore and prepare for certain paths, and of course, colleges want to get to know you, so they care about the paths you have explored.
